Texas isn't just home to ducks ‚Äî it's also a winter home for thousands of geese! These birds fly incredibly long distances, traveling all the way from Canada and Alaska to spend the winter in Texas where the weather is warmer and food is easier to find.

üåü What Makes Geese Special?

  • They're bigger than ducks. Some geese can weigh 14 to 20 pounds ‚Äî that's as heavy as a small dog!
  • They molt once a year. Molting means they shed old feathers and grow new ones.
  • Their legs are farther forward on their bodies than ducks. This helps them walk and graze on land, eating grasses, grains, and plants.
  • They still have webbed feet and flat bills, just like ducks, which help them swim and eat.
  • Baby geese (called goslings) hatch covered in soft down feathers and can walk or swim within hours of being born.
  • Geese are the second‚Äëlargest waterfowl, right behind swans.

Geese in Texas are often grouped into light geese and dark geese, based on their coloring.
